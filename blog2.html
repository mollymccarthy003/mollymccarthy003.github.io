Getting Started in Ceramics: A Beginner-Friendly Guide

Published on December 7, 2025

Ceramics is one of those art forms that looks intimidating from the outside but is incredibly grounding once you dive in. Clay invites you to get messy, experiment and learn through doing. There are so many options for beginners to dive into, like hand building or throwing on the wheel. I took my first ceramics class last year at Madison Area Technical College and fell in love with it. Now I am in my second ceramics class and considering changing my degree into the field.


Ceramics is all about understanding the material. Clay responds to pressure, moisture and temperature which makes your hands your most important tools. You don’t need to start with fancy equipment and most beginners start with hand building.

There are so many great Youtube videos and online content for beginners to use. There are also many community colleges that offer ceramics classes for an all-inclusive price.


If you are interested in the pottery wheel, it does take practice, but I find it to be incredibly meditative. By applying equal pressure until it spins smoothly you center the clay. This is the hardest part. Once centered, you can pull the clay upward and shape it into cylinders, bowls or cups! To get started, most new ceramics artists need just a few tools. There are many affordable kits at craft stores.


Shaping the clay is only the beginning, because ceramics is a multiple step process. Once your clay piece dries from leather hard to bone dry, place it on the bisque shelf to be fired for the first time in the kiln. There are many local pottery studios that offer artists a kiln fee for firing. Once you get your piece back you can glaze it and fire again. Different glazes react differently with other glazes and certain glazes have to be fired at a certain temperature. Make sure to double-check what cone your kiln will be fired to. You could even create some test tiles. Because these pieces sometimes take weeks from start to finish. This timeline builds anticipation and appreciation for the craft.


Working with clay is a rewarding process that teaches patience, problem-solving and flow. Your first pieces won’t be perfect and that is the beauty of it. Each wobbly mug and wonky bowl holds memories of your progress! I am so happy that I took a chance on this new medium, I have created some of my favorite art pieces!

If you’ve been curious about ceramics, take this as your sign to try it out. Sign up for a community class, watch a Youtube video or borrow your artsy friends tools. You might just discover your new favorite creative outlet.
